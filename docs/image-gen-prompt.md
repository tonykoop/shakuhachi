# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A single end-blown Japanese-style shakuhachi flute presented as a finished, real physical object: a slender straight cylindrical tube roughly the length of a forearm, turned from a solid billet of warm reddish-brown cherry hardwood with visible fine straight grain and a hand-rubbed satin oil finish, not bamboo and not lacquered. The defining feature is the utaguchi — a clean beveled crescent notch cut into the rim of the top (blowing) end, where the player's lip rests; it reads as a single oblique chamfer carved into the wall, optionally fitted with a small flush dark inlay block at the cut edge. The body is a smooth simple profile with no keys, no fipple, no whistle window, no mouthpiece — just the open notched top end and a plain squared open foot at the bottom. Down the front face sit four small round finger holes and one thumb hole on the back, drilled clean, no hardware. Realistic scale cues from being held upright like a recorder. Clean neutral light-grey studio background, soft directional studio light raking across the grain, three-quarter product-photography angle at about 35 degrees from horizontal, ~50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

The same solid-cherry shakuhachi resting on a luthier's workbench in a warm wood shop, staged mid-build alongside a long straight-flute reamer and a chromatic tuner, soft natural light from a north-facing window raking across shavings and the satin-oiled body, shallow depth of field with the beveled utaguchi notch in crisp focus and the bench tooling falling soft behind. Environmental storytelling of a Western luthier-shop adaptation of a traditional instrument. Concept visualization only.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the utaguchi: the beveled crescent notch carved into the rim of the blowing end of a solid cherry hardwood tube, showing the single clean inner bevel angle, the gently rounded outer edge, the smooth bore wall behind the cut, and the optional flush dark inlay block seated at the cutting edge. Raking side light to reveal the hand-filed bevel surface, the wood grain, and the satin oil sheen. High detail, photorealistic, no measurements, no angle callouts.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension lines, angle callouts, or tuning numbers; no bamboo nodes/segments or split-cane construction; no fipple, whistle window, or recorder mouthpiece; no keys or metal mechanism; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
